Phase 4 ‚Äî RETIREMENT

Tax-Advantaged Wealth for the Long Game

Your retirement accounts are the most powerful wealth-building vehicles available. Understanding contribution limits, tax treatment, and account types can save you hundreds of thousands over a lifetime.

The Optimal Order of Operations

1
Get the full 401(k) employer match
This is an instant 50‚Äì100% return on your money. Always take the free money first.
2
Max out your HSA (if eligible)
Triple tax advantage ‚Äî contributions pre-tax, growth tax-free, withdrawals tax-free for medical. The best account in the US tax code.
3
Max out your Roth IRA
Tax-free growth forever. Best for younger investors in lower tax brackets. $7,000/yr limit (2026).
4
Max out 401(k) to the full $23,500
Reduces taxable income today. Grows tax-deferred. 401(k) limits are much higher than IRA limits.
5
Invest in taxable brokerage (LATER strategies)
Unlimited contributions. Full flexibility. Use tax-efficient ETFs (low turnover, qualified dividends).
üå±

Roth IRA

$7,000 / yr

Contribute after-tax dollars. Everything inside grows 100% tax-free ‚Äî dividends, capital gains, everything. Withdrawals in retirement are completely tax-free. Best for anyone under 50 who expects to be in a higher tax bracket later.

  • Income limit: $161K single / $240K married (phases out)
  • Contributions (not gains) withdrawable penalty-free anytime
  • No required minimum distributions (RMDs)
  • Best funds inside: FZROX, VTI, QQQ, SPY
üè¢

Traditional 401(k)

$23,500 / yr

Pre-tax contributions lower your taxable income today. Grows tax-deferred until retirement. Get your full employer match before doing anything else ‚Äî it's instant 50‚Äì100% return.

  • Employer match: typical 3‚Äì6% of salary
  • Traditional: pay taxes at withdrawal (lower bracket in retirement)
  • Roth 401k option: post-tax, tax-free withdrawal
  • Best funds: low-expense index funds (S&P 500, Total Market)
üè•

Health Savings Account (HSA)

$4,300 / yr

The best account in the US tax code. Pre-tax contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical. After age 65, withdraw for anything (taxed like Traditional IRA). Must have an HDHP health plan to contribute.

  • Triple tax advantage (contribute ‚Üí grow ‚Üí withdraw tax-free)
  • Invest in index funds once balance exceeds $1K‚Äì$2K
  • Save receipts ‚Äî reimburse yourself years later, tax-free
  • Never expires ‚Äî rolls over every year
üìö

529 Education Savings

No annual limit

Tax-advantaged savings for education expenses. Contributions made after-tax. Growth and withdrawals are tax-free when used for qualified education expenses (tuition, room, board, books).

  • State income tax deduction in most states
  • Can change beneficiary to another family member
  • Up to $35K can roll into a Roth IRA (SECURE 2.0)
  • Best for: children, grandchildren, your own education
üéØ

Traditional IRA

$7,000 / yr

Pre-tax contributions (if deductible) with tax-deferred growth. Good if you don't have a 401k at work or are in a higher tax bracket now vs. expected retirement bracket.

  • Deductibility phases out with workplace 401k access
  • Required Minimum Distributions starting at age 73
  • Backdoor Roth strategy available for high earners

Real Estate + REITs

Unlimited

Real estate exposure without owning property. REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts) are required to distribute 90%+ of taxable income as dividends. Good inflation hedge and income stream.

  • VNQ ‚Äî Vanguard REIT ETF (diversified real estate)
  • O ‚Äî Realty Income (monthly dividend "bond alternative")
  • AMT ‚Äî American Tower (cell towers, data centers)
  • Hold in Roth IRA ‚Äî REIT dividends taxed as ordinary income
